Current is charge in motion. It is defined to be the in the direction of positive charge flow.

Current can described by its density:
$$
J = \sum_{k}n_kq_ku_k
$$
$$
J_e=-eN_e\bar{u}_e = \rho_e\bar{u}_e
$$
The current $I$ flowing through a surface $S$ is
$$
I = \int_{S} J\cdot da
$$
Steady current is when vector $J$ remains constant throughout a system.

For any closed surface, a truly time dependent current will result in the above integral being zero. Equivalently,
$$
\nabla \cdot J = 0.
$$
Current can also be written as 
$$
-\frac{dQ}{dt}=I
$$
