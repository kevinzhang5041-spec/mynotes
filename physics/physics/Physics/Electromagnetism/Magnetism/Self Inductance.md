  When a [[Current|current]] $I_1$ is changing, there is an [[Electromotive Force|emf]] $\mathcal{E}_{11}$ through the [[Circuits|circuit]] itself.
$$
\mathcal{E}_{11} = - \frac{d\phi_{11}}{dt},
$$
and with 
$$
L_1=\frac{\phi_{11}}{I_1} \equiv \text{constant},
$$
$$
\mathcal{E}_{11} = - L_1\frac{dI_1}{dt}.
$$
These statements do not hold under a rapidly changing $I_1$.
