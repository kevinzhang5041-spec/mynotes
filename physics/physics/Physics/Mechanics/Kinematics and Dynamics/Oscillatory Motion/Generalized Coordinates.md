If we can express kinetic energy $K$ and potential energy $V$ as
$$
K = \frac{1}{2}m_{\text{eff}} \, \dot{q}^2, \, \, \, V = \frac{1}{2}k_{\text{eff}} \, q^2,
$$
the oscillation frequency is always 
$$
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k_{\text{eff}}}{m_{\text{eff}}}}.
$$
We can also use 
$$
k_{\text{eff}} = V''(q_{\text{min}}).
$$
Related to [[Euler - Lagrange]] and [[Central Forces]].

More specifically, any potential function will follow
$$
U(r) \approx  U(r_0) + \frac{1}{2}U''(r_0)(r-r_0)^2,
$$
acting like a spring force.
