Fact: $PV^\gamma = \text{(constant)}$
Proof: For an adiabatic process, there is no heat exchange between the body and the environment throughout the process, meaning $dQ = 0$. Therefore, $dU = dQ + dW = dW$, so $$\begin{align*} n C_v \, dT &= - P \, dV \\ n R \, dT \cdot \frac{1}{\gamma - 1} &= - P \, dV \\ \frac{PV}{T (\gamma - 1)} dT &= - P \, dV \\ \frac{dV}{V} &= -\frac{dT}{T} \cdot \frac{1}{\gamma - 1} \end{align*}$$Integrating both sides gives $$\ln V = -\frac{1}{\gamma - 1} \ln T + C \implies V^{\gamma - 1} \propto \frac{1}{T}.$$Finally, using the fact that $T \propto PV$ by the Ideal Gas Law, we see $$PV \cdot V^{\gamma-1} = PV^{\gamma} \propto 1 \implies \boxed{PV^{\gamma} = \text{(constant)}}$$
