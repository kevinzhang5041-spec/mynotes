Orbits follow [[Central Forces]] and [[Kepler's Laws]]. 

For a general orbit, the total energy is 
$$
E = - \frac{GMm}{2a}
$$
This applies to hyperbolas (negative $a$), and the parabola (infinite $a$) where total energy vanishes.

Closed orbits only exist if the total energy is negative (cannot escape to infinity).

Circular orbits exist when $V_{\text{eff}}' = 0$.

Where 
$$V_\text{eff} = V(r) + \frac{L^2}{2mr^2}$$
Orbits can undergo [[Simple Harmonic Motion]], specifically where $V_{\text{eff}}$ is a minimum.

Virial Theorem:
For bound orbits, the time-averaged values of the kinetic and potential energy are related by 
$$
\langle K \rangle = -\frac{1}{2} \langle V \rangle.
$$
This holds for other bound orbits where $V(r) \propto r^n$, where
$$
\langle K \rangle = \frac{n}{2} \langle V \rangle.
$$

This then implies
$$v^2=GM(\frac{2}{r}-\frac{1}{a})$$
