Using results from the [[Differentials for Varying Forces|processes]], we can solve for our equations. 

If a function is proportional to its second derivative, there is a good chance that that the solution is in the form
$$
x(t) = Ae^{\alpha t} + Be^{-\alpha t}.
$$
Essentially guess that $x = Ae^{\alpha t}$ or $Ae^{i\omega t}$ (in this case only keep the real parts) and solve. use [[Euler's Formula]] as needed.

The sum of solutions to a linear differential equations is another solution. This is called the principle of superposition.
