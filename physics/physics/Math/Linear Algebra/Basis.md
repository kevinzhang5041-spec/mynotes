A set of vectors is a basis if it is linearly independent and spans the vector space.
$$
\text{Basis}=\{\text{linearly independent}\}+\{\text{spanning}\}
$$

Linear independence
$$
c_1v_1+c_2v_2+\cdots+c_nv_n=0
$$

implies
$$
c_1=c_2=\cdots=c_n=0
$$


Spanning

Every vector in the space can be written as a linear combination of the basis vectors.

$$
v=c_1v_1+c_2v_2+\cdots+c_nv_n
$$


Basis vectors are in the vector space

$$
v_1,v_2,\dots,v_n\in V
$$


Number of vectors in a basis is the dimension

$$
\dim(V)=n
$$


Coordinates relative to a basis

$$
[v]_B=
\begin{bmatrix}
c_1\\
c_2\\
\vdots\\
c_n
\end{bmatrix}
$$
