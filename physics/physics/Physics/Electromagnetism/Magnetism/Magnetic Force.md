Due to a current $I = \lambda_0 v_0$ a single charge will experience a force
$$
F_y = \frac{qv_xI}{2 \pi \epsilon_0 r c^2} = \frac{qv_xI}{2 \pi \,r \mu_0} = \vec{v} \times \vec{B}.
$$
This is because a current running wire in the lab frame has no net charge, but the frame is shifted to the one of a moving charge there is a difference in the linear density of positive charge and negative charge (because one of the two is moving in the lab frame).

In general the force on a wire from the [[Magnetic Field|]]is 
$$
dF = I dl \times B.
$$
