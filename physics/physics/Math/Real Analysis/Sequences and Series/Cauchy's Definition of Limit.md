#### (a) Convergence to a finite limit

A sequence $(x_n)$ converges to $L$ if and only if  
for every $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists $N = N(\varepsilon)$ such that for all $n > N$,  
$$  
|x_n - L| < \varepsilon.  
$$

Equivalently:  
$$  
x_n \to L \iff \forall \varepsilon>0, \exists N, \forall n>N:\ |x_n-L|<\varepsilon.  
$$

---

#### (b) Divergence to infinity

A sequence $(x_n)$ tends to $+\infty$ if for every $\varepsilon > 0$, there exists $N = N(\varepsilon)$ such that for all $n > N$,  
$$  
x_n > \varepsilon.  
$$

Equivalently:  
$$  
x_n \to +\infty \iff \forall \varepsilon>0, \exists N, \forall n>N:\ x_n>\varepsilon.  
$$
