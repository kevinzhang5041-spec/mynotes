From [[Maxwell's Equations]], we can describe a wave of [[Electric Field|electric fields]] and [[Magnetic Field|magnetic fields]] with
$$
v = \pm\frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\epsilon_0}} = \pm c
$$
and 
$$
E_0 = \pm \frac{B_0}{\sqrt{\mu_o\epsilon_0}} = \pm cB_0.
$$
The two fields are perpendicular.

The power density, or energy flow per unit area, is given by 
$$
S = \epsilon_0 \overline{E^2} \, c.
$$
The energy transfer can be described by the Poynting vector:
$$
\textbf{S} \equiv\frac{\textbf{E} \times \textbf{B}}{\mu_0},
$$
$$
\frac{du}{dt} = -\int_S \textbf{S} \cdot d\textbf{a}
$$
