Under the influence of an radial force, the value of [[Angular Momentum|angular momentum]] $L$ remains constant.
$$
\frac{d}{dt}(mr^2 \dot{\theta})=0.
$$
This allows us to define an effective potential $V_{\text{eff}}(r)$.
$$
V_{\text{eff}}(r) = \frac{L^2}{2mr^2} + V(r).
$$
A circular orbit exists when the first derivative of the above is $0$. This is taken from 
$$
\frac{1}{2}m\dot{r}^2 + \frac{L^2}{2mr^2} +V(r) = E.
$$
Another useful re-expression of this is
$$
\biggl( \frac{1}{r} \frac{dr}{d\theta} \biggr)^2 = \frac{2mE}{L^2}-\frac{1}{r^2}- \frac{2mV(r)}{L^2}

$$


To be able to escape, there needs to be a maximum to "bounce" off of to reach infinity.
