In a changing [[Magnetic Field|magnetic field]], a loop of wire will generate a [[Electromotive Force|emf]] to counteract the change in flux. This is called Lenz's law.

Faraday's law states that for a surface $S$ enclosed by a curve $C$, we have that the emf is 
$$
\mathcal{E} = \int_{C} E \,\cdot ds = - \frac{d}{dt}\int_{S} B \, \cdot da = -\frac{d\phi}{dt},
$$
or 
$$
\nabla \times E = - \frac{\delta B}{\delta t}.
$$
