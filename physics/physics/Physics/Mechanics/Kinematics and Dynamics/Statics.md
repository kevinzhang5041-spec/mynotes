For an object to remain in static equilibrium, the net [[Newton's Laws|force]] and net [[Physics/Electromagnetism/Mechanics/Kinematics and Dynamics/Rotation/Torque|torque]] on a body must equal $0$. 

The center of mass's acceleration must also be $0$. Written out mathematically,
$$
\sum_{i=1}^{n}m_ia_i=0.
$$

Given three torques on a body, the lines on the vectors of said torques must intersect at a single point if the body is static.
![[Torque Static]]
The point of concurrency does not necessarily have to be on the body.

For a body to be static, its [[Momentum|center of mass]] must lie directly below or above the contact point with respect to a force (i.e. gravity).

A different perspective is that if a system is in static equilibrium it must experience no net virtual work under any displacement, including rotations, so the torques must cancel.
