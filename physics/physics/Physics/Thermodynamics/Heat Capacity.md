Heat Capacity $C$ is defined as 

$$
dQ = nC_V \, dT \  \ \ \text{(constant volume)}
$$
$$
dQ = nC_P \, dT \  \ \ \text{(constant pressure)}
$$
Where
$$
\gamma = \frac{C_P}{C_V} = \text{adiabatic coefficient}
$$
$$C_V=\bigg(\frac{\delta Q}{\delta T}\bigg)_V=\bigg(\frac{\delta U}{\delta T}\bigg)_V =\frac{1}{\gamma -1 } R
$$
$$
C_P=C_V + R = \bigg(\frac{\delta U}{\delta T}\bigg)_V+\bigg[\bigg(\frac{\delta U}{\delta V}\bigg)_T+p\bigg]\bigg(\frac{\delta V}{\delta T}\bigg)_p = \frac{\gamma}{1-\gamma}
R$$
