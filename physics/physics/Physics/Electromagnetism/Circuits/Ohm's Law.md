Conductivity $\sigma$ is the linear factor between [[Current|current density]] and [[Electric Field|electric field]]. Conductivity is dependent on the material.
$$
J = \sigma E,
$$
Where $\sigma$ can be approximated as 
$$
\sigma \approx e^2(\frac{N_+\tau_+}{M_+}+\frac{N_-\tau_-}{M_-})
$$
A consequence is
$$
V = IR,
$$
where $R$ can be calculated from
$$
R = \frac{\rho L}{A},
$$
where $\rho$ is resistivity.

This relates to conductivity $\sigma$ by 
$$
\sigma =\frac{1}{\rho}.
$$
