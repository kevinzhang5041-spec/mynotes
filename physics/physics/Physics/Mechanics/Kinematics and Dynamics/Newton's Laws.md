First Law:
Every object will remain at rest or in uniform motion unless acted on by a force. This is true for any frame/

Second Law:
$$F=ma=\frac{dp}{dt}$$
In the absence of other forces,  a force $F$ on a mass $m$ will cause it to accelerate at a rate of $a$.

Third Law:
Every force has an equal and opposite reaction.

An equivalent statement is that the total [[Momentum|momentum]] of a system is conserved. This can be expressed as
$$
\frac{dp_{total}}{dt} =\frac{dp_{1}}{dt}+\frac{dp_{2}}{dt} = F_1 + F_2
$$
