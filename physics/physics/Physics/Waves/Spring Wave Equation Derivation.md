Let $x$ be the position of a point on a spring relative to the origin and $S(x, t)$ be the wave function defined relative to the origin. ![[Spring Wave Equation Derivation 2024-08-06 13.46.48.excalidraw]]
The acceleration of this point is $\frac{\delta^2 S}{\delta t^2}$ by definition.

The spring constant of an elemental segment of the spring of length $dx$ is $\frac{kL}{dx}$. Therefore, since the point travels length $\delta \ell = S(x+dx)-S(x)$, the restoring force at $x$ is $$F = \frac{kL}{dx} \delta l = \frac{kL}{dx} [S(x+dx)-S(x)] = kL \frac{\delta S}{\delta x}.$$ The net restoring force experienced by mass $dm = \mu dx$ is $$F_{net}=  kL \frac{\delta S}{\delta x} |_{x+dx} - kL \frac{\delta S}{\delta x} |_x = kL \frac{\delta^2 S}{\delta x^2} dx.$$ Then, $$kL \frac{\delta^2 S}{\delta x^2} dx = \mu dx \frac{\delta^2 S}{\delta t^2}.$$ Dividing both sides by $dx$ gives the wave equation, $$\boxed{\frac{\delta^2 S}{\delta x^2} = \frac{1}{v^2} \frac{\delta^2 S}{\delta t^2}},$$ where $v = \sqrt{\frac{kL}{\mu}}$.
