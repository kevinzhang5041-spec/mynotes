Simple harmonic motion (SHM) is a system that oscillates between states because of a restoring force. Examples include [[Spring Block Systems|spring block systems]], and [[Pendulums|pendulums]], and [[Angular Kinematics|circular motion]].

Equations for Hooke's law type force:
$$
x(t)=A\cos(\omega t+ \phi)
$$
$$
x'(t)=-A \, \omega \sin(\omega t)
$$

If a system boils down to $\ddot{z} + \omega^2z=0$, the system under goes SHM with a frequency equal to the square root to the coefficient of $z$, no matter what it is (in the example it is $\omega$). Note that the coefficient should be positive.

Constant terms can be ignored.
