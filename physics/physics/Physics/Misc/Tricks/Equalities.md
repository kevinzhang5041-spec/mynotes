For two objects to stay in contact (for a normal force to exist), the original setup's conditions must stay in continuity. 

Examples:

3.8 Morin
A block of mass $m$ is held motionless on a frictionless plane of mass $M$ and angle of inclination $θ$ . The plane rests on a frictionless horizontal surface. The block is released. What is the horizontal acceleration of the plane?
![[morin 3.8.png]]
The horizontal distance between the block and its starting point on the plane is $\frac{(a_x+A_X)t^2}{2}$, and the vertical distance is $\frac{a_yt^2}{2}$. The ratio of these distances must equal $\tan(\theta)$ if the block is to remain on the plane (imagine looking at things in the frame of the plane). 
Therefore, we must have $\frac{a_y}{a_x + Ax} = \tan(\theta)$.
