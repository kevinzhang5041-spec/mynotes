A magnetic dipole with moment $m = IA$ its field is similar to [[Electric Dipole]], given by
$$
B(r) = \frac{\mu_0m}{4\pi r^3}(2\cos(\theta) {\hat{r}} + \sin(\theta){\hat{\theta}}) = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi r^3}(3(m \cdot \hat{r})\hat{r}-m)
$$

The force on a dipole is given by

$$
\vec{F} = (\vec m \cdot \nabla)\vec B
$$
and torque is
$$\vec \tau = \vec m \times \vec B$$
and "potential energy" of 
$$U= - \vec m \cdot \vec B$$
