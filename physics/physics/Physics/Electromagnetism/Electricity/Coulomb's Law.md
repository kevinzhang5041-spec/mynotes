Coulomb's Law states that for two point charges with charges $q_1$ and $q_2$, the force that each charge experiences from each other is given by
$$
F_e = k \frac{q_1 q_2}{d^2} = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{q_1 q_2}{d^2}
$$
where $d$ is the distance between the two charges. 

Negative values of $F_e$ indicate an attractive force, positive values indicate an repulsive force.

The fact that $F_e$ is determined by $\frac{1}{d^2}$ allows the use of [[Gauss's Law]] and its consequences.
