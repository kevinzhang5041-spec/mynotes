Torque is the rotational equivalent of [[Newton's Laws|force]]. Statements concerning force can be translated into statements containing torque.

1. An body will stay at rest or in rotation at a constant speed unless a torque is applied.
2. Torque $\tau = I\alpha$, where $I$ is the [[Moment of Inertia|moment of inertia]] and $\alpha$ is [[Angular Kinematics|angular acceleration]].
3. A third law analogue is hard to define.

Torque is calculated as 
$$\vec{\tau} = \vec{F} \times \vec{r},$$
and 
$$\tau = F\,r\sin(\theta),$$
where $\theta$ is the angle that the force makes with the lever.

The net torque on a body needs to be $0$ for it to be [[Statics|static]].
