Judges the entirety of Electromagnetism.
$$
\nabla \times \textbf{E} = -\frac{\delta\textbf{B}}{\delta t}
$$
$$
\nabla \times \textbf{B} = \mu_0 \, \epsilon_0\frac{\delta\textbf{E}}{\delta t} + \mu_0\textbf{J}  
$$
$$
\nabla \cdot \textbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}
$$
$$
\nabla \cdot \textbf{B} = 0
$$
Where the first is [[Inductance|Faraday's Law of Inductance]], the second expresses the dependence of the [[Magnetic Field|magnetic field]] on the [[Displacement Current|displacement current]] density (simplifies to [[Ampere's Law]]), the third is an equivalent form of [[Coulomb's Law]] (or [[Gauss's Law]]).
