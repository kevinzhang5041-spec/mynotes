Really, these are only used for first order approximations of common trigonometric and exponential functions. The most common are listed below.

For $\theta <<1$,
$$\begin{align*}
	\sin \theta \approx \tan \theta \approx \theta \\
	\cos \theta \approx 1 - \frac{\theta^2}{2} \approx \theta
\end{align*}$$
A derivative of Taylor Series (or binomial expansion) is **Bernoulli's Inequality**, which for $a > 0$ and $n > 1$ states that
$$(1+ax)^n \geq 1+ax \cdot n$$
Often times these can be used to approximate trigonometric functions when given ratios of lengths, for example $(1+x)^{1/2} \approx 1 + \frac{x}{2}.$
