A geometric series has the form $$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^n=a+ar+ar^2+\cdots$$

- Converges if $|r|<1$
    
- Diverges if $|r|\ge1$
    
- If convergent,  
    $$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^n=\frac{a}{1-r}$$
    
- If starting at $n=k$,  
    $$\sum_{n=k}^{\infty} ar^n=\frac{ar^k}{1-r},\quad |r|<1$$
    
- Common strategy: rewrite terms to identify the common ratio $r$
