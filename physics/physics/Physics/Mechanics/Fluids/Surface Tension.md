Surface tension $\gamma$ is defined as 
$$
\gamma = \frac{dW}{dA} = \frac{U}{A},
$$
$$
dF=\gamma \, ds \times \vec{n}.
$$
meaning tension acts along the surface and perpendicular to a cut. This is the relation between potential energy of a surface to its area.

Young-Laplace equation states that for a spherical surface 
$$
\Delta P = \frac{2\gamma}{R}.
$$
Adjust appropriately based on context. For a bubble, the RHS is doubled as there is an inner and outer component.  

This is the force that balances out a pressure difference

For a general surface,
$$
\Delta P = \gamma (\frac{1}{R_1}+\frac{1}{R_2})
$$
Where $R$ is the principle radii of curvature.
