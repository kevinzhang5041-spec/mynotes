#### (a) Convergence case

If  
$$  
a_n \le b_n \le c_n \quad \text{for all } n,  
$$  
and  
$$  
a_n \to L,\quad c_n \to L,  
$$  
then  
$$  
b_n \to L.  
$$

**Idea:** if both bounds converge to the same limit, anything trapped between them must converge to that same limit.

---

#### (b) Divergence to infinity

If  
$$  
a_n \le b_n \quad \text{for all } n,  
$$  
and  
$$  
a_n \to +\infty,  
$$  
then  
$$  
b_n \to +\infty.  
$$

**Idea:** if a lower bound goes to infinity, anything above it must also go to infinity.
